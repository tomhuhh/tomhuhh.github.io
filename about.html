About

Background

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Animal Science, with a minor in Systems Engineering, at Cornell University, advised by Drs. Kristan F. Reed and Thomas R. Overton, and I expect to complete my degree in December 2026. My long-term objective is to become an independent researcher leading interdisciplinary work at the intersection of animal agriculture, sustainability, data science, and decision support — developing practical tools that improve environmental performance, productivity, and resilience in livestock systems.

Before Cornell, I earned an M.S. in Dairy Science at the University of Wisconsin–Madison, and a B.S. in Animal Science jointly through China Agricultural University and Cornell University.

Research focus

My doctoral work centers on the Ruminant Farm Systems (RuFaS) model, an integrated whole-farm simulation platform for US dairy systems developed as an open-source project. My research spans field measurement — using sensor networks and ventilation-based flux methods to quantify barn ammonia emissions — and model development, integrating that field data into RuFaS through data assimilation. I have also led a methodological comparison between RuFaS and COMET-Farm (a widely used greenhouse-gas accounting tool), directed a multi-laboratory evaluation of manure nutrient analyses, and contributed automated ration-formulation logic to the model. My work has informed RuFaS's role as the modeling engine behind Version 3 of the dairy industry's FARM Environmental Stewardship Program.

Industry & applied experience

Between my M.S. and Ph.D., I worked at C-Lock Inc. in Rapid City, SD, progressing from Junior to Lead Data Analyst. There I processed and validated ruminant gas-emissions data from GreenFeed systems, trained and supervised junior analysts, led code reviews, and represented the company at field deployments and technical events — experience that grounds my academic modeling work in the realities of commercial data collection.

Beyond the model

As a Foundation for Food & Agriculture Research (FFAR) Fellow (2023–2026) — one of 30 fellows selected nationally — and a Cornell Green Technology Innovation Fellow (2025–2026), I have conducted 50+ stakeholder interviews with farmers, advisors, and industry professionals to understand real-world barriers to adopting decision-support tools. I served as Professional Development and Operations Manager for the Cornell Animal Science Graduate Student Association (2024–2025).

Longer term, I am interested in AI-assisted agricultural modeling — including large language models and agent-based workflows — as a way to make whole-farm systems modeling faster to build, easier to validate, and more useful for the people who run dairy operations day to day.

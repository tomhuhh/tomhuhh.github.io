About

Background

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in Animal Science (minor in Systems Engineering) at Cornell, advised by Drs. Kristan Reed and Thomas Overton, finishing December 2026. I build whole-farm environmental models for dairy — bridging barn-level field measurement and simulation to make sustainability tools more accurate and decision-ready.

  • Education M.S., Dairy Science, UW–Madison · B.S., Animal Science, China Agricultural University & Cornell
  • Industry Lead Data Analyst, C-Lock Inc. — GreenFeed ruminant emissions data
  • Fellowships FFAR Fellow, 2023–26 (1 of 30 nationally) · Cornell Green Technology Innovation Fellow, 2025–26

Research focus

My work centers on the open-source Ruminant Farm Systems (RuFaS) whole-farm dairy model. I quantify barn ammonia emissions with sensor networks and ventilation-based flux methods, then assimilate that field data into RuFaS — and I've compared RuFaS with COMET-Farm, directed a multi-laboratory manure-analysis study, and added automated ration formulation to the model.

Long term, I aim to lead interdisciplinary research across animal agriculture, sustainability, data science, and decision support — building practical tools that improve performance, productivity, and resilience in livestock systems.

About

Background

Haowen Hu is a Ph.D. candidate in Animal Science, with a minor in Systems Engineering, at Cornell University, where he is advised by Drs. Kristan F. Reed and Thomas R. Overton and expects to complete his degree in December 2026. His long-term objective is to become an independent researcher leading interdisciplinary work at the intersection of animal agriculture, sustainability, data science, and decision support — developing practical tools that improve environmental performance, productivity, and resilience in livestock systems.

Before Cornell, Haowen earned an M.S. in Dairy Science at the University of Wisconsin–Madison, advised by Dr. Sebastian Arriola-Apelo, and a B.S. in Animal Science jointly through China Agricultural University and Cornell University, completing an honors thesis under Dr. Michael Van Amburgh.

Research focus

Haowen's doctoral work centers on the Ruminant Farm Systems (RuFaS) model, an integrated whole-farm simulation platform for US dairy systems. His research spans field measurement — using sensor networks and ventilation-based flux methods to quantify barn ammonia emissions — and model development, integrating that field data into RuFaS through data assimilation. He has also led a methodological comparison between RuFaS and COMET-Farm (a widely used greenhouse-gas accounting tool), directed a multi-laboratory evaluation of manure nutrient analyses, and contributed automated ration-formulation logic to the model. His work has informed RuFaS's role as the modeling engine behind Version 3 of the dairy industry's FARM Environmental Stewardship Program.

Industry & applied experience

Between his M.S. and Ph.D., Haowen worked at C-Lock Inc. in Rapid City, SD, progressing from Junior to Lead Data Analyst. There he processed and validated ruminant gas-emissions data from GreenFeed systems, trained and supervised junior analysts, led code reviews, and represented the company at field deployments and technical events — experience that grounds his academic modeling work in the realities of commercial data collection.

Beyond the model

As a Foundation for Food & Agriculture Research (FFAR) Fellow (2023–2026) — one of 30 fellows selected nationally — and a Cornell Green Technology Innovation Fellow (2025–2026), Haowen has conducted 80+ stakeholder interviews with farmers, advisors, and industry professionals to understand real-world barriers to adopting decision-support tools. He also teaches: guest-lecturing on RuFaS and dairy sustainability in Cornell and Colgate University courses, and supporting instruction in dairy herd management and data science applications in agriculture. He currently serves as Professional Development and Operations Manager for the Cornell Animal Science Graduate Student Association.

Longer term, Haowen is interested in AI-assisted agricultural modeling — including large language models and agent-based workflows — as a way to make whole-farm systems modeling faster to build, easier to validate, and more useful for the people who run dairy operations day to day.

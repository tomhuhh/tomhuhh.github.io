About

Background

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Animal Science, with a minor in Systems Engineering, at Cornell University, advised by Drs. Kristan F. Reed and Thomas R. Overton; I expect to finish in December 2026.

Before Cornell, I earned an M.S. in Dairy Science at the University of Wisconsin–Madison and a B.S. in Animal Science jointly through China Agricultural University and Cornell University.

Research focus

My doctoral work centers on the Ruminant Farm Systems (RuFaS) model, an open-source, integrated whole-farm simulation platform for US dairy systems. It spans field measurement — quantifying barn ammonia emissions with sensor networks and ventilation-based flux methods — and model development, integrating that field data into RuFaS through data assimilation. I have also led a methodological comparison between RuFaS and COMET-Farm (a widely used greenhouse-gas accounting tool), directed a multi-laboratory evaluation of manure nutrient analyses, and contributed automated ration-formulation logic to the model. My work has informed RuFaS's role as the modeling engine behind Version 3 of the dairy industry's FARM Environmental Stewardship Program.

Industry & applied experience

Between my M.S. and Ph.D., I worked at C-Lock Inc. in Rapid City, SD, rising from Junior to Lead Data Analyst. I processed and validated ruminant gas-emissions data from GreenFeed systems, trained and supervised junior analysts, led code reviews, and represented the company at field deployments and technical events — experience that grounds my modeling work in the realities of commercial data collection.

Beyond the model

As a Foundation for Food & Agriculture Research (FFAR) Fellow (2023–2026), one of 30 selected nationally, I have completed 150+ hours of professional development workshops; and as a Cornell Green Technology Innovation Fellow (2025–2026), I have conducted 50+ stakeholder interviews with farmers, advisors, and industry professionals on real-world barriers to adopting decision-support tools. I served as Professional Development and Operations Manager for the Cornell Animal Science Graduate Student Association (2024–2025) and organized the first Cornell Women in Animal Science Panel.

My long-term goal is to lead independent, interdisciplinary research at the intersection of animal agriculture, sustainability, data science, and decision support — building practical tools that improve performance, productivity, and resilience in livestock systems.
